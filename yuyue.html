In Summer 2018, I was a visiting student and research assistant in Artificial Intelligence Lab, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, advised by Prof. Bo Li.

Currently, I am fortunate enough to be a full-time research intern at City Brain Research Center in Hangzhou, China with the topic of Intelligent Traffic Signal Control via Deep Reinforcement Learning under the supervision of Prof. Zhenhui(Jessie) Li.

My research interests lie in data mining, ubiquitous computing, complex information network analysis and recommender systems. I am both interested in discovering new problems and developing scalable and efficient algorithms on topics mentioned above.

Characterizing Malicious Edges Targeting on Graph Neural Networks
Xiaojun Xu*, Yue Yu*, Bo Li, Le Song, Chengfeng Liu and Carl Gunter.
Jul. 2018 - Sep. 2018, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.
  • Designed a novel graph generation method together with link prediction method to detect malicious edges. We also leveraged novel features to perform outlier detection for detection.
  • Extensive experiments showed that the proposed detection mechanism can achieve AUC above 90% against attack strategies on both Cora and Citeseer datasets.
  • Key Words: Adversarial Machine Learning, Graph Mining, Graph Neural Networks.
Understanding Urban Dynamics via State-sharing HMM
Tong Xia*, Yue Yu*, Fengli Xu and Yong Li.
Jun. 2018 - Nov. 2018, Tsinghua University.
  • We designed a novel urban dynamic revealing system based on HMM to identify the typical dynamic patterns on various regions of the city with different urban functions.
  • Our model addressed the challenge of data sparsity by allowing different regions to share parameters, which fully exploited the correlation between regions.
  • Evaluation on real-world PoI check-in data and mobility flow data in Beijing demonstate that our model outperforms 54.2% than traditional HMM model on reconstruction error.
  • Key Words: Urban Computing, Spatial-Temporal Data Mining, Hidden Markov Models.
Privacy-preserving Cross-domain Location Recommendation
Chen Gao, Chao Huang, Yue Yu and Yong Li.
May. 2018 - Aug. 2018, Tsinghua University.
  • We presented a new framework for cross-domain location recommendation, in which data from the auxiliary domain is protected via our obfuscation mechanisms.
  • We designed a novel solution named confidence-enhanced collective matrix factorization (CCMF) to infer the confidence level of the location in auxillary domain to improve the recommendation performance overall.
  • We evaluated our model using real-world dataset collecting from Foursquare and Tencent and the PoI recommendation performance was elevated by 2.05%-111.76%.
  • Key Words: Collaborative Filtering, Location Recommendation, Data Privacy.
B&B: Planning bus routes with sharing-bikes
Yue Yu, Tong Xia and Yong Li.
Dec. 2017 - May. 2018, Tsinghua University.
  • We detected the hot spots for urban traffic and formulated the candidate bus stations via mining the massive trajectory data.
  • We designed a heuristic approach to expand each bus routes w.r.t. the sharing-bikes by proposing a function to determine whether a road segment should be designed as bus route or sharing-bike lanes.
  • The evaluation on real-world dataset of taxi and sharing-bike services in New York City demonstrates that our method outperforms 7.05% and 15.55% over the baseline methods without sharing-bikes.
  • Key Words: Bus route design, Sharing-bikes, Urban planning.
